Choices for developing

I prefer to develop not listening to any music. However, as it happens, my neighbors tend to be very loud and disrupt my focus. Because of this, my investment into a pair of nice quality headphones has paid off. My genres of choice are 'Ambient' and 'Downtempo'. These are my top artists to listen to when developing:

Definitely my favorite artist for developing work. His music aids my natural flow of though, without interrupting my concentration. I think the title of his first album - Drum Therapy - is a nice description of what he is trying to achieve with his music. It is relaxing and nourishing.

Choices for puzzling

I like to do a few sudoku puzzles per day. My genres of choice are 'Soundtrack' and 'Classical Piano'. These are some artists I like to listen to while puzzling:

Max Richter

As a cinematic composer, he makes frames come alive with his emotional music. The reason I love his pieces so much is because, no matter where I am, or what I am doing, his music adds to the perceived significance of it.

'The Departure' on YouTube
Nils Frahm

As one of my favorite albums of all time, Nils Frahm's 'Felt' is, for me, a masterpiece. The way he builds song upon song, with seamless transitions, he can make my perspective change easily. In my opinion, he is one of the most talented pianists and composers of our time.

'More' on YouTube

Choices for relaxing

When I am relaxing, I prefer the spoken word. However, I do want to mention that not all Hip Hop is created equal. My taste consists mostly of jazzy beats and rhythmic flows. One of my all time favorite artists is called 'A Tribe Called Quest', and the following two tracks are by them:

Electric Relaxation

There is not much to say about this one. It encapsulates all of the elements I like about ATCQ so much: perfect transitions between Q-Tip and Phife Dawg, atmospheric background sounds that can easily make you fall into a musical trance, and lyrics that are a perfect fit for the beat.

'Electric Relaxation' on YouTube
Stressed Out

While Electric Relaxation is my perfect song in beats and melody, Stressed Out is my perfect song in rhymes and flow. I love how casual and relaxed they rap, yet their message still comes through with force. This is one of the main reasons why I love them: their music is about balance.

'Stressed Out' on YouTube
